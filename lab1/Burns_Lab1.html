For the non-continuous section of the lab, I will be examining the binomial distribution. Binomial distributions are discrete because the values of the possible outcomes, or the values that are being measured, can be thought of as being true or false. In other words, p defines the bias of true vs. false, n is the total number trials, and k represents the number of 'trues' if you run n trials. So the outcome is the probability of getting k 'trues' in any order, with the probability of being true or false defined by p, when performing n trials. So the results will be discrete because you can't have a value of 4.25 trues, they must be whole numbers like 4 trues. The following is the probability mass function for the binomial.

$f(k)=\begin{pmatrix} n\\ k \end{pmatrix}p^k(1-p)^{n-k}$

D. Because the distributions are discrete, so are the probabilities and 'sigmas'. Think about this and discuss the implications for experiments.

Given that the distribution is discrete, and thus the sigma values must be too, this implies that you should be able to analyze the data with greater certainty, because if the values for sigma and the probability don't match known discrete values, there must be a problem with the data.

E. While the results are discrete, the parameters of the distributions are not. For example, the mean of a Poisson distribution can be 9.2. Think about and explain why this makes sense and what changes continuously and what doesn't.

Discrete distributions can and most likely will produce parameters that are not discrete simply because of the way the numbers can be manipulated. To look at the example described above, a Poisson distribution can have a mean that is not a discrete value because it takes the sum of n integers and divides them by n. To further communicate this idea, think of a discrete distribution comprised of 3 values, 9, 7, and 6, their sum is 22 and 22 divided by 3 is 7.33333, in other words, not a discrete value. So the data, sigma, and probilities will all be confined to discrete values, and things like the mean, and standard deviation will be continuous.
